Tommy Tutone, a 1980s rock band best known for their top 5 song “867-5309/Jenny,” was founded by Thomas Allen “Tommy” Heath (born March 31, 1947), who is also the main vocalist, rhythm guitarist, and sometimes keyboardist of the band. It is a widespread misunderstanding that Tommy Tutone is the name of the main vocalist; in fact, it is the name of the rock band Tommy Tutone. To differentiate themselves from other bands, Tommy Tutone changed the name of his band from “Tommy and the Tu-tones.” He departed the band following the publication of their third studio album, 1983’s National Emotion, which was his last record with them. Nervous Love, a 1994 album that featured songs produced after his Tommy Tutone days, was his first major studio release since his Tommy Tutone days. He went on to work as a computer analyst and relocated to Portland, Oregon. He now lives in Santa Rosa, California, with his wife and two children. On June 9, 2008, while on vacation in Lake Tahoe, California, he tied the knot with his long-term love, Lisa Scholtz.
