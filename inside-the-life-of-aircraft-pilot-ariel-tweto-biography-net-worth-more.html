Who is Ariel Tweto?

Ariel Tweto, born Areil Eva Tuadraq-Aauchaq Tweto on December 14, 1987, is an American. She and her sister Ayla were raised in Unalakleet, Alaska, in the Nome Census Region. Ariel went to East High School, then Emerson College, and finally Chapman University. Ariel Tweto, the daughter of Ferno and Jim Tweto, had a fortunate upbringing because she was born into an industrious family.
The Tweto managed a family airline, which explains Ariel’s fascination with flight.

Ariel was an obviously daring young parent who learned a lot from her Alaska-based company, which mostly ferries people and things through the frigid zone. Ariel has strong family values, despite the fact that she no longer resides in her hometown. She shares a very cordial relationship with her sister Ayla and does not hesitate to visit her.

Tweto’s outstanding performance on her first show did not go unnoticed, and other possibilities followed quickly. Her next television project was a collaboration with Discovery Channel reality, who promised to create a show based on her family’s aircraft business. As a result, Flying Wild Alaska was born in 2011. The Discovery Channel show followed and filmed the family business Tweto, which transports products and people across rural Alaska, as well as Areil’s aviation adventures. On April 21, 2012, the Flying Wild Alaska celebrity received her pilot’s license after multiple drills and flight training under the supervision of an FAA examiner.
