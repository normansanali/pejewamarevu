Meet Comedian Janelle James, The Host Of The Final Straw

ABC premiered a new game show on 10 July 2022 titled The Final Straw. Every episode has four teams of two people competing to remove one object at a time from a massive tower until it becomes unstable enough to tumble when the other teams do the same. Each episode is a self-contained competition with a three-round main game and a final round, the Mega Stack. Each team can earn up to $250,000.

Moving on to the main reason for this article, comedian Janelle is the host of the show. “The Final Straw is a fresh and exciting format that you truly have not seen anywhere else. It’s a simple concept with big visuals that is sure to keep audiences on their toes and entertain families all summer long,” said Rob Mills, executive vice president, Unscripted and Alternative Entertainment, Walt Disney Television. “With Janelle’s spot-on comedic timing and Peyton’s knack for competition, we have the perfect duo to launch ABC’s newest unscripted series.”

“I’m thrilled to be part of a series that is as silly and fun as I am. I’m looking forward to this opportunity to grow my relationship with ABC with my second comedy project on the network,” Janelle commented about getting to host the show.”

How Much Is Comedian Janelle James Net Worth?

Working from both New York and Los Angeles, Janelle James wears many hats. She is an American comedian, writer, and actress.

In 2009, Janelle started her career at an open mic she went to while residing in Champaign, Illinois. She describes her brand of humor as “simply talking shit” and her stand-up routines cover a variety of subjects, including dating, parenthood, Trump, and being a woman over 35. Bill Burr, Hadiyah Robinson, John Early, Wanda Sykes, Richard Pryor, and Kareem Green were mentioned by James as some of his favorite comics.

Janelle had grown up in the Virgin Islands, worked in fashion PR, attended Cepheid T. Fashion Institute of Technology top fashion school located in New York, and was living in Illinois when she first walked on stage at an open mic night at the age of 30. But as soon as she hit the stage, she knew this was her calling and many years later she’s headlining clubs all over the country.

Along with others, Janelle has performed on tour with Chris Rock, Amy Schumer, and David Cross. Janelle assumed control of the Eugene Mirman Comedy Festival in Brooklyn, which is now known as the Janelle James Comedy Festival. She was voted one of the 50 Funniest People in Brooklyn in 2016 and was a part of the Just for Laughs Montreal New Faces category for 2016.

Janelle was most recently named by Variety as one of the Top 10 Comedians to Watch in 2020. In September 2017, “Black and Mild,” her debut album, was made available on all major streaming services. James has written for and appeared in the television shows “Black Monday” on Showtime, “Central Park” on Apple TV+, and “Abbott Elementary” on ABC.

Meanwhile, her acting projects also include Tom of Your Life, Sixteen Thousand Dollars, Crashing, Dare Dorm 20, Life & Beth, and all the three aforementioned shows she wrote for.
