What paint did Van Gogh use?

Publish date: 2024-08-11

Van Gogh primarily used oil paint for his artworks. He favored this medium due to its flexibility and vibrant colors, which allowed him to achieve the expressive and textured brushwork for which he is famous. Oil paint consists of pigments mixed with a drying oil, typically linseed oil, and it dries slowly, allowing artists to work with the paint for longer periods and make revisions as needed. Van Gogh experimented with various brands and types of oil paints, using both commercially available tubes and hand-ground pigments mixed with oil.In addition to traditional oil paint, Van Gogh occasionally incorporated unconventional materials into his artworks. One notable example is his use of powdered pigment known as impasto. This technique involved mixing sand or other granular substances directly into the paint, creating a thick and textured surface. By doing so, Van Gogh added an unexpected physicality to his paintings, emphasizing the tactile qualities and making his works visually distinctive. This unconventional use of materials added depth and intensity to his paintings, further enhancing his unique artistic style.
